Objective:

We are conducting an academic survey aimed at evaluating the quality of AI-generated motions. Your input is essential in helping us understand how well the generated motions follow the provided instructions and maintain natural quality. This survey will guide us in improving the AI's ability to create lifelike movements and align with specific editing instructions.

Survey Overview:

You will be shown a source motion alongside an edited motion. Your task is to evaluate both based on specific criteria. After evaluating a few pre-selected examples, you will also be asked to rate several edited motions generated from the same source motion but using different methods. The survey is split into multiple pages, with each page containing one scenario. You can navigate through the pages using "Next" or "Previous" buttons, and you can only proceed after completing all the required fields on the current page.

Evaluation Criteria:

For each pair of videos (source and edited), you will be asked to evaluate based on the following:

- Content Similarity: Does the edited motion maintain the original content of the source motion?
Example rating scale:
1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree)

- Alignment with Instruction: Does the edited motion follow the editing command accurately?
Example rating scale:
1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree)

- Motion Quality: Is the quality of the edited motion good and does it appear natural?
Example rating scale:
1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree)

Survey Structure:

The survey is divided into two sections:

- Evaluation of Pre-selected Motion Examples:
In the first section, you will evaluate a few hand-picked examples of edited motions. Each example will have its own source and edited video pair. Rate the content similarity, alignment with instruction, and the quality of motion for each video pair. Evaluation of Randomly Selected Motion Samples: In the second section, you will be presented with 5 randomly selected scenarios. For each scenario, five different edited motions will be displayed, each generated using different methods. You will be asked to rate each generated motion based on the same criteria (content similarity, alignment, and motion quality).

- Instructions:
Start by reviewing the pre-selected motion examples: Each page contains a scenario description, an editing instruction, and two videos: the source motion and the edited motion. After watching the videos, use the provided radio buttons to rate each pair on the three criteria. Click "Next" to proceed to the next example. Evaluate randomly selected scenarios: For each scenario, you will be presented with five different edited motions. Review all the videos and rate each of them based on the same criteria as in the first section. You can navigate between scenarios using "Next" and "Previous" buttons.

- Completion:
Once you have completed all the evaluations, click "Submit" to finish the survey.

Tips:

Make sure you review the entire motion in both videos before making your decision. If you're unsure about a rating, select "Neutral" to indicate a balanced evaluation. All required fields must be completed before you can move to the next page or submit your results.
